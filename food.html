Boston, as an internationalized metropolis, has a great variety of restaurants. However, the restaurant industry has went through an unprecedented hard time because of COVID-19. As the pandemic is more under control than past few months, our Hermes Food's local experts scour Boston again for the new best restaurants. Here are the new list of best restaurants in Boston! Eating in Boston

Boston is filled with amazing neighborhoods, and one of the apsects of any noteworthy region is its quality and abundance of dining options. For those in Boston, whether they are young college students, first time visitors, or long-standing residents, consider some of the neighborhoods below to expand dining options

Back Bay

Not only is Back Bay one of the nicest places to live in Boston, it is filled with a variety of high end shops and restaurants to satisfy any newcomer. Filled with a number of bars, grills, and luxury establishments, it is hard to not find a place to purge one of their food desires.

North End

Known as Little Italy, this neighborhood is situated right next to the city's harbor. It contains a bevy of Italian-themed attractions, including restaurnats, bakeries, coffee shops, and other attractions. A lively place to visit with friends and family, the North End serves as one of the most popular destinations for tourists and others alike

Fenway/Kenmore

Not far from Boston University's campus, the neighborhood revolving around historic Fenway Park represents an additional place to explore with great company. Home to a number of sandwich shops, grills, and burger joints, the Fenway area symbolizes an area of relaxation and comfort food, as well as a convenient location to go see the Red Sox play.

Boston's Finest Restaurants

Oishii Boston

One of the city's hardest-to-get-into Japanese restaurants. Oishii Boston located in the South End SOWA district, the most artistic area in Boston. The restaurant is devoted to providing the most wonderful dining experience to every customer. Chef Ting San is dedicated to combining every dish with the freshest ingredients and creativity, to transform a dish into a work of art.
Recommended Food:

  • Foie Gras With Truffle
  • Sakura Smoked Hamachi
  • Toro Tartare

Bar 'Cino

Located in the South Campus of Boston University, Bar 'Cino offers students and others a chance to gain insights of Boston's Italian cuisine without having to go to the North End.

In particular, Bar 'Cino offers a lively setting, as the restaurant is typically filled with many young people during the weekends.

Some of the best things to try on the menu include the following:

  • The bolognese pasta
  • The assortment of pizzas
  • The various small plates that can be shared with others

Santouka Back Bay

One of the many offerings of delicious Ramen in Boston, Santouka is located not far from the Hynes Convention Center in Back Bay. It serves as a tasty addition for anyone visiting downtown.

It is recommended that one tries any of the following when visiting:

  • Shio ramen
  • Miso ramen
  • The different side dishes, including those with cheek meat and dumplings.

Bova's Bakery

A delicious way to end the day, this North End destination offers customers a variety of deserts to conclude their dining.

Its location in one of the most popular parts of the city further serves it as a great place to visit with friends.

Try any of the following:

  • Cannolis
  • Cakes
  • Cookies

Neptune Oyster

It’s the place to sample requisite local essentials—raw oysters, lobster rolls, and fried clams—with a guarantee of freshness and an absence of kitsch.
Recommended Food:

  • Marine Lobster Roll
  • Sea Urchin Ditalini
  • Wellfleet Clam Chowder

Sumiao Hunan Kitchen

Sumiao Kitchen, a Chinese restaurant that serves distinctively spicy, sweet, and sour dishes. Characterized by bright colors, bold flavors, and a diverse palate, Sumiao introduces great Hunan cuisine, one of the eight famous culinary traditions of China.
Try these:

  • Sweet Sticky Rice Cake
  • Duck Yolk Breaded Crispy Shrimp
  • Hot Kiss
